As a family of four, we recently visited Bangkok and had an amazing time exploring the city's top attractions. Here are our top five recommendations for families visiting Bangkok:

1. Chatuchak Weekend Market: If you love shopping, this is the place to be. With over 15,000 stalls, Chatuchak Weekend Market is one of the largest markets in the world. You can find everything from clothing and accessories to home decor and souvenirs. Our kids loved the food stalls, where they tried delicious Thai street food like Pad Thai and mango sticky rice.

2. Grand Palace: The Grand Palace is a must-visit attraction in Bangkok. This stunning complex of buildings is the former residence of the Thai King and is now a museum. The architecture is breathtaking, with intricate details and vibrant colors. Our kids were fascinated by the Emerald Buddha, a small statue made of jade that is considered one of the most sacred Buddha images in Thailand.

3. Wat Pho: Wat Pho is another temple complex that is worth visiting. It is home to the famous Reclining Buddha, a 46-meter-long statue that is covered in gold leaf. The temple complex is also known for its traditional Thai massage, which is a great way to relax after a long day of sightseeing. Our kids loved exploring the temple grounds and taking in the beautiful scenery.

4. Jim Thompson House: Jim Thompson was an American businessman who helped revive the Thai silk industry in the 1950s and 1960s. His house, which is now a museum, is a beautiful example of traditional Thai architecture. The house is surrounded by lush gardens and ponds, and our kids loved exploring the grounds and learning about Thai culture.

5. Bangkok National Museum: If you're interested in history and culture, the Bangkok National Museum is a great place to visit. The museum has an extensive collection of artifacts and exhibits that showcase Thailand's rich cultural heritage. Our kids loved the interactive exhibits, where they could try on traditional Thai costumes and learn about the different regions of Thailand.

Overall, we had an amazing time in Bangkok and would highly recommend it as a family-friendly destination. There is so much to see and do, and the city has a vibrant energy that is contagious. We can't wait to go back and explore more of what Bangkok has to offer!

(I am a 40-year-old parent)
