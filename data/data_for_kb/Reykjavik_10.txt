As a mother of two young children, I recently had the pleasure of visiting Reykjavik, Iceland, and I must say, it was an unforgettable experience. Here are some of the top attractions and activities that we enjoyed during our trip:

1. The Blue Lagoon: This geothermal spa is a must-visit for anyone traveling to Iceland with children. The warm, mineral-rich water is perfect for soothing tired muscles after a long day of exploring, and the kids loved playing in the shallow areas and trying out the silica mud masks. We also enjoyed the on-site restaurant, which offered a variety of healthy and delicious meal options.

2. The Reykjavik Zoo and Family Park: This park is a great place to take young children, with a variety of animals to see, including reindeer, Arctic foxes, and Icelandic horses. The kids also loved the playgrounds, petting zoo, and pony rides. We spent a full day here and barely scratched the surface of all there was to see and do.

3. The Perlan Museum: This museum is a great way to learn about Iceland's natural history and culture. The kids loved the interactive exhibits, including a simulated glacier hike and a virtual reality experience that took us on a journey through Iceland's geological history. We also enjoyed the panoramic views of Reykjavik from the top of the museum's observation deck.

4. The Reykjavik City Card: This card is a great value for families visiting Reykjavik, as it offers free admission to many of the city's top attractions, including the zoo, the Perlan Museum, and the National Museum of Iceland. It also includes free public transportation, which made it easy to get around the city with the kids in tow.

5. The Golden Circle Tour: This tour takes you to some of Iceland's most iconic natural attractions, including the Geysir geothermal area, Gullfoss waterfall, and Thingvellir National Park. The kids loved seeing the geysers erupt and the waterfall up close, and we all enjoyed learning about Iceland's history and culture along the way.

Overall, we had a fantastic time in Reykjavik and would highly recommend it as a family-friendly destination. There is so much to see and do, and the city is very welcoming to families with young children. We can't wait to go back and explore more of this beautiful country!

I am a 35-year-old mother of two young children.
